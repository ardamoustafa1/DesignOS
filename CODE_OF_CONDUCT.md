# Code of Conduct

DesignOS's review culture is adversarial toward *work* and respectful toward *people* —
the reviewer agent attacks findings, never authors. The community holds the same line.

## The honesty doctrine (applies here, too)

DesignOS's core ethos is "measured, not vibed" and "say what's true, not what lands
 well." That doctrine doesn't stop at design outputs — it shapes how we treat each other:

- **Every opinion earns its place with reasoning.** "I prefer X" without a why is the
  same non-contribution in a conversation as it is in a module PR. Reasoning keeps the bar
  high and makes feedback genuinely useful.
- **Negative results are as valuable as positive ones.** If DesignOS didn't help your
  project, say so. Honest negative reports (like `evals/field-report-001.md`) improve the
  system; suppressed negatives inflate a false success story.
- **Concede when you're wrong — fast.** The scoring rubric's scoring loop closes with
  a finding, not a defense. Conversations here should too.

## Expected
- Critique with specifics and reasons (the `CONTRIBUTING.md` evidence standard applies
  to conduct in reviews: "this violates spacing.md's 2× rule because…" — never "this is
  garbage")
- Assume competence; disagree with rules by challenging them through the rule-challenge
  issue template, with evidence
- Welcome newcomers: the bar is high for *rules*, not for *questions*

## Not tolerated
- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind
- Dismissing contributions by identity rather than content
- Bad-faith participation (spam, self-promotion floods, LLM-generated filler PRs)
- Dark patterns in contributions — inflated claims, cherry-picked results, or hiding
  limitations are the same violations in community behavior as they are in design work

## Enforcement
Maintainers may edit, close, or remove contributions and contributors that violate this.
Report conduct issues privately via GitHub's report mechanisms or the security contact
in `SECURITY.md`.

Adapted in spirit from the Contributor Covenant v2.1; extended to reflect DesignOS's
honesty-first design philosophy.

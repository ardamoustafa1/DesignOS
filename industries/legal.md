# Industry: Legal (Firms & Legal Tech)

## Buyer psychology
Legal clients arrive in trouble or avoiding it — divorce, dispute, deal, compliance. The
emotional register is **worry seeking authority**: they're hiring judgment, and every
pixel is scanned for competence signals (`psychology/trust.md` craft-transfer at maximum).
For legal tech, add a second skeptic: lawyers themselves, trained to find the flaw in
any claim — precision of language IS the product demo.

## Visual language
- **Color:** deep navies, charcoals, forest greens; restrained gold/burgundy accents for
  heritage firms; legal tech may modernize (clean blues, warm neutrals) but never toward
  playful. Whitespace = confidence; density = desperation (a cramped firm site reads as
  a cramped firm).
- **Type:** serif display is the sector's native authority (Freight/Tiempos class) +
  neutral sans body; legal tech: disciplined grotesque throughout. Typography errors are
  disqualifying here — the audience reads contracts for a living.
- **Imagery:** real attorneys (professional, consistent portrait system —
  `patterns/company-pages.md` team doctrine), real offices; NO gavel/scales-of-justice
  stock (the sector's handshake-lightbulb), no marble columns unless you own them.

## Page priorities
1. **Practice areas** — the IA backbone: one page per area, plain-language problem
   framing ("Buying a company?" before "M&A Advisory"), attorney contacts per area
2. **Attorney profiles** — the sector's product pages: photo, credentials (bar, courts,
   education), representative matters (as permitted), publications, direct contact —
   clients hire people, firms invoice
3. **Results/matters** — case results with the required disclaimers ("results don't
   guarantee outcomes"), anonymized where ethics demand
4. **Contact/intake** — consultation CTA with expectation-setting (fees for consult?
   response time?); intake forms: minimal, confidentiality-noted, NEVER ask case details
   in a web form (privilege isn't established — say so: "please don't include
   confidential details")
5. Legal tech: security/privilege page (`industries/cybersecurity.md` trust-center
   doctrine — privileged data raises the bar beyond SOC2)

## Trust requirements
Bar admissions, court admissions, credentials verifiable · recognitions only if real
(Chambers, Legal 500 — the audience knows which badges are bought) · client logos/matters
only as ethics rules permit per jurisdiction · **the disclaimer layer designed, not
dumped**: attorney-advertising notices, no-attorney-client-relationship notices — present,
readable, but architecturally quiet (13px organized footer blocks, not mid-page shouting).

## Compliance design notes
Attorney advertising rules vary hard by jurisdiction (testimonials banned in some,
"specialist" claims regulated) — flag every superlative and testimonial for counsel
review in `memory/todo.md`. Accessibility matters doubly: firms get sued for their own
inaccessible sites, and the irony is fatal.

## Motion character
Calm-authoritative preset: fades over movement, zero springs, zero decoration. Speed
still matters (`psychology/trust.md` — slow sites read as inefficient counsel).

## Anti-patterns
Gavel/scales/handshake stock · "aggressive advocates fighting for YOU" copy inflation ·
countdown urgency anywhere · testimonial walls in regulated jurisdictions · fee opacity
where clients expect ranges (consumer practices increasingly publish; opacity reads as
ambush) · legal tech demoing with real-looking case data.

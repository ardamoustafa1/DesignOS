# Industry: Portfolio & Agency

## Buyer psychology
The site IS the product sample. A visitor hiring a designer/developer/agency evaluates one
thing: *"if they do this for themselves, what will they do for me?"* — every detail is
audition footage. Buyers scan in this order: **work → who → process → contact**, and they
decide from the work grid in under 30 seconds. Secondary audience: peers/recruiters (hiring
full-time) who dig deeper into case studies.

## Visual language
- **One signature move, executed perfectly,** beats ten trends sampled: a distinctive type
  choice, a color voice, an interaction — pick the ONE thing that's yours (`brain/design-intelligence.md`
  signature-moment budget) and keep everything else disciplined.
- **Color:** the anti-SaaS palette space is open here — warm neutrals + serif editorial, stark
  black/white, or one loud personal accent. Whatever it is: committed, consistent, yours.
- **Type:** the strongest personality lever — editorial serifs, expressive display faces
  licensed (readability floors still apply). Type IS the brand for text-led portfolios.
- **Imagery:** work shots art-directed as products themselves — consistent mockup language,
  real-device frames or full-bleed crops, NO stretched screenshots, no default browser-frame
  screenshots from staging URLs.

## Page priorities & patterns
1. **Home = work grid** (or work-forward hero): 4–8 best projects, each card = key visual +
   client + one-line what-you-did; the grid's craft (spacing, hover, image quality) is the
   first competence proof. One-line positioning statement above ("Brand systems for climate
   companies") — specific positioning converts; "I make beautiful experiences" doesn't.
2. **Case studies — the conversion engine:** problem → constraints → process (real artifacts:
   sketches, iterations, rejected directions — process transparency sells senior work) →
   solution (big, plentiful, captioned) → **results with numbers** → team/role honesty
   ("my role: interaction design; branding by X"). 3 deep beats 12 shallow.
3. **About:** real photo, real voice, capabilities stated concretely, client list, press/awards
   (real), the human reason to pick you.
4. **Contact:** friction-free — email visible (no form-only walls), availability status
   ("Booking March"), what-happens-next for agencies ("Reply within 24h → 30-min call").
5. **Agency additions:** services (named, scoped — vague "digital solutions" repels), team
   (real people), engagement model hints (process page, even pricing signals — "projects from
   $25k" filters perfectly).

## Trust requirements
Real clients confirmable · results with numbers where possible · testimonials with full names,
roles, faces · currency (recent work visible — a 2019-latest portfolio asks "what happened?") ·
the site's own performance (a slow portfolio for a "performance-focused developer" is
self-refutation — `checklists/performance.md` at the sector's strictest).

## Motion character
The playground — custom cursors, page transitions, hover reveals, scroll choreography all
licensed HERE — under the audition rule: every trick must be flawless (60fps, reduced-motion,
keyboard-safe) because broken-fancy is worse than plain. One coherent motion voice, not a
demo reel of everything you can do.

## Anti-patterns
Skill bars (85% Photoshop = amateur badge) · stock imagery ANYWHERE · "we craft pixel-perfect
experiences" copy soup · NDA-everything grids ("trust me" doesn't screenshot) · loaders on a
6-page site · contact forms with 9 fields · dead blog with 2 posts from 2022 (delete it) ·
imitating the currently-viral portfolio (juries and clients have seen it 50 times this month).

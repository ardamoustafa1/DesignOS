# Industry: Nonprofit & Government/Civic

Two audiences share this file's physics: organizations spending OTHER people's money
(donors' or taxpayers') for public good. The design consequence is identical:
**stewardship must be visible** — every flourish is scrutinized as waste, every
obscurity as evasion.

## Buyer psychology
- **Donors** give emotionally and verify rationally: story opens the wallet, transparency
  keeps it open (impact numbers, program-vs-overhead honesty, ratings). Recurring donors —
  the real asset — are retained by *impact receipts*, not repeat asks
  (`psychology/habit-retention.md` value-receipt doctrine).
- **Civic users** don't choose to be here: they need the permit, the benefit, the form —
  often stressed, often on old devices, spanning every literacy and ability level.
  Government UX is accessibility + plain language or it is exclusion.

## Visual language
- **Color:** hopeful-but-serious — deep blues/greens/warm earth tones; nonprofits may
  carry one warm energetic accent (donate buttons own it exclusively); government:
  institutional restraint, semantic clarity, zero decoration competing with wayfinding.
- **Type:** maximum-legibility humanist sans, body 17–18px+, plain-language sizing for
  stressed readers (`industries/healthcare.md` doctrine); reading level 6th–8th grade
  ENFORCED for civic content (many governments mandate it — GOV.UK style guides).
- **Imagery:** dignity doctrine — real people served, photographed as protagonists, never
  as pity props (poverty-porn destroys modern donor trust and violates subjects);
  consent-documented; government: photography sparse, iconography functional.

## Nonprofit patterns
1. **The donate flow is the product** (`patterns/pricing.md` + `industries/ecommerce.md`
   checkout doctrine): preset amounts with impact anchors ("$50 = school supplies for
   one child" — honest math only), monthly-first framing (recurring is the sector's
   annuity; default respectfully, never pre-check), guest-fast, Apple/Google Pay,
   fee-coverage option honestly framed, receipt immediate + tax-deductible details.
2. **Impact transparency:** the numbers page — program ratio, annual reports designed
   (not PDF-dumped), outcome metrics with methodology; charity-rating badges
   (Charity Navigator etc.) where strong.
3. **Story architecture:** one protagonist beats statistics for opening hearts
   (`psychology/persuasion.md` — the identifiable-victim effect), statistics beat stories
   for justifying scale; sequence: story → mechanism → scale numbers → ask.
4. Volunteer/advocacy funnels: distinct CTAs, never buried under donate.

## Government/civic patterns
1. **Task-first IA:** the homepage is a service directory ranked by demand ("Renew
   license", "Pay taxes", "Report issue") + search — never an org chart or a mayor's
   photo-op carousel (GOV.UK's revolution: start with user needs, delete everything else).
2. **Forms at maximum forgiveness** (`components/forms.md` + `components/steppers-wizards.md`):
   save-and-resume mandatory (sessions expire, lives interrupt), plain-language questions,
   "why we ask" per field, document-upload with `components/file-upload.md` mercy,
   confirmation numbers + printable/email receipts.
3. **Status transparency:** application tracking ("received → reviewing → decision",
   dates honest — `components/states.md`), service-status pages for outages.
4. **Multilingual as architecture,** not afterthought: language switch persistent,
   translations professional, RTL-ready where relevant.

## Trust requirements
Registration/tax-status numbers visible · financials findable in ≤2 clicks · SSL/privacy
posture stated at data entry · .gov/.org domain hygiene · security contact
(`industries/cybersecurity.md` disclosure doctrine applies to civic infrastructure).

## Motion & performance
Calm preset, near-zero decoration; performance budget at the STRICTEST tier
(`checklists/performance.md`) — old devices, slow networks, and metered data are the
primary audience, not the edge case. Accessibility: WCAG AA is legally mandated for
government in most jurisdictions; treat AAA as the target (`foundations/accessibility.md`).

## Anti-patterns
Pity-prop imagery · donation popups ambushing first-time readers · overhead-shame
("100% goes to the cause" — someone pays the lights; honesty beats theater) · carousel
homepages of press releases · PDF-only civic information · CAPTCHA walls before benefit
applications · session-expired form wipes (civic cruelty, and common).

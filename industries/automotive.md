# Industry: Automotive

## Buyer psychology
A car is the second-largest purchase and a full identity statement — bought over WEEKS
of configurator-dreaming, spec-comparing, and dealer-dreading. Three surfaces, three
psychologies: **brand/model sites** (desire manufacturing — the sector invented
aspiration marketing), **the configurator** (the play-and-commit machine), and
**marketplaces/dealers** (trust archaeology: "what's wrong with this specific car?").
EV-era addition: the buyer is often tech-evaluating (range math, charging, software)
as much as car-shopping.

## Visual language
- **The vehicle IS the design system:** full-bleed cinematic photography/renders,
  dramatic lighting, environments matched to positioning (mountain = SUV freedom,
  night city = performance EV); the car renders BIG (`brain/reference-library.md`
  Apple product-scale doctrine — automotive taught Apple, reclaim it).
- **Color:** near-monochrome chrome lets paint colors own the page; luxury: black/
  charcoal + metallic restraint; EV brands: clean light minimalism (Tesla-established)
  or warm-technical counters.
- **Type:** confident grotesques, oversized numerals for THE stats (range, 0-100,
  charging minutes — `foundations/typography.md` display-numbers craft; spec typography
  is the sector's data-viz).
- **Motion:** the licensed cinematic sector — scroll-driven reveals, rotating vehicles,
  paint-color transitions — under `motion/page-and-scroll.md` law: fallbacks total,
  scroll never hijacked, LCP guarded (`checklists/performance.md` — a 40MB hero video
  loses the phone-browsing majority).

## Page priorities & patterns
1. **Model page:** one hero claim + THE three numbers → cinematic scroll story
   (exterior → interior → tech → safety) → spec table (expandable, honest) → CTA pair
   ("Configure" + "Test drive") — the test-drive booking is the REAL conversion; treat
   its form with `components/forms.md` minimalism.
2. **The configurator — the sector's crown jewel:** live vehicle view updating per
   choice (paint/wheels/interior — `components/pickers.md` swatch doctrine at maximum:
   show the color ON the car), running total ALWAYS visible (tabular, honest, financing
   toggle — `patterns/pricing.md` transparency), steps per
   `components/steppers-wizards.md` (safe back, save/share/resume the build — shared
   configs are the sector's viral loop), summary → deposit/dealer handoff with
   what-happens-next.
3. **EV-specific surfaces:** range calculator (honest: speed/weather/temperature
   inputs — overpromised range is the category's trust wound), charging-network maps,
   total-cost-vs-ICE math with sourced assumptions.
4. **Marketplace/dealer listings:** `industries/ecommerce.md` PLP/PDP doctrine +
   vehicle-history transparency (accidents, owners, service records — the
   trust-archaeology answer), 360°/undercarriage photos, inspection reports designed
   (`patterns/print-documents.md`), price-vs-market context.

## Trust requirements
Real specs with test-condition footnotes (WLTP/EPA stated) · safety ratings displayed
(NCAP/IIHS) · transparent pricing (destination fees, the dreaded dealer-markup
question addressed) · availability honesty (build timelines, not fantasy delivery
dates) · recalls/service handled gracefully in owner surfaces
(`psychology/trust.md` breach-and-repair).

## Motion character
Confident-cinematic on brand surfaces (one signature scroll story per model page);
configurator: responsive-precise (paint transitions 300ms, price updates instant);
owner/service portals: calm-operational (`industries/insurance.md` claims register —
a recall notice is a claims moment).

## Anti-patterns
Autoplay engine-roar audio · configurators that lose the build on back · monthly-price
anchoring hiding the real total (`psychology/persuasion.md` honest-anchor rule) ·
range figures without conditions · scroll-jacked 19-section stories on mobile ·
dealer sites drowning the car in badge-spam and popup chat · stock-photo interiors
for a specific used listing.

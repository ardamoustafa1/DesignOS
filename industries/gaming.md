# Industry: Gaming

## Buyer psychology
Players buy **feelings in advance**: the site's job is to transmit the game's emotional
register (dread, power, coziness, speed) before a single gameplay second. The audience is
media-literate and allergic to marketing-speak — they trust gameplay footage, not adjectives.
Two distinct products share this file: **game sites** (emotion-first) and **gaming
tools/platforms** (Discord-class — community-energy + product clarity).

## Visual language
- **The game's art IS the design system:** the site extends the game's palette, type voice, and world — a cozy farming sim and a tactical shooter must not share a template. Extract tokens from the game art (`memory/brand.md`).
- **Color:** high contrast, saturated accents licensed here like nowhere else; dark themes dominate (screenshot/footage luminance pops on dark).
- **Type:** display faces with personality allowed (ONE, for headings; UI text stays disciplined and legible — HUD discipline: expressive shell, readable core).
- **Imagery:** gameplay footage > cinematic trailer > key art > screenshots > text. Video hero muted-autoplay with poster fallback and data-respect (`motion/performance.md`).

## Page priorities & patterns
1. **Hero:** key art or gameplay loop + title treatment + ONE action (Wishlist/Buy/Download/Play) + platform icon row — wishlist-on-Steam is the pre-launch conversion, treat the button with primary reverence
2. **Feature/world sections:** show mechanics via clips-in-cards; lore/world sections may go full editorial-immersive (the one sector where scroll-storytelling earns its cost — with the static fallback rule)
3. **Media section:** screenshot gallery (lightbox, keyboard-safe), trailers, presskit link (press/creators are first-class users — a real presskit page: logos, art, fact sheet, contact)
4. **Community strip:** Discord CTA with live member count (real), socials, UGC showcases
5. **System requirements / platforms / age rating:** the diligence layer — tabled, complete, findable

## Trust requirements
Real gameplay labeled as such ("in-engine" vs "cinematic" — the audience punishes bait) ·
review scores/steam rating honestly quoted · roadmap honesty for early access (delivered vs
planned visually distinct) · age ratings displayed correctly per market.

## Motion character
The maximum-license sector — springs, parallax depth, glow, particles ALL available — under
three hard rules: (1) 60fps or cut it (`motion/performance.md` — this audience notices frame
drops professionally), (2) reduced-motion fallback total, (3) the shell never upstages the
footage: when video plays, chrome freezes.

## Platform/tool variant (Discord/Steam-class)
Community energy in marketing + ruthless product clarity in-app: dense friend lists/libraries
follow `components/` doctrine with the brand's expressive skin; dark-first; latency = credibility.

## Anti-patterns
Template-swap sites where the game's soul is absent · autoplay WITH sound · "epic/immersive/
stunning" adjective soup · fake in-game screenshots (bullshots) · countdowns to countdowns ·
requiring login to see system requirements · seizure-adjacent strobe sections (WCAG hard line —
`foundations/accessibility.md`).

## References

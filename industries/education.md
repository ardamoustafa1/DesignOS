# Industry: Education (EdTech, Courses, Institutions)

## Buyer psychology
Learners buy **a future version of themselves** — the design sells the outcome (career,
skill, grade), then must immediately support the grind (progress, momentum, not-feeling-
stupid). Frequent split: the *learner* uses it, a *parent/employer/admin* pays — pages per
audience, and the payer needs outcome evidence (completion rates, placements, curriculum depth).
Motivation is the retention problem: every returning screen must answer "where was I, and
how far have I come?"

## Visual language
- **Color:** optimistic but credible — friendly blues/greens/warm accents on light ground; K-12 may go brighter (never clownish); higher-ed/professional certs stay institutional (navy, deep green, serif gravitas). Duolingo-bright only for genuinely gamified consumer products.
- **Type:** highly legible humanist sans; body 17–18px for reading-heavy surfaces; long-form content typography is core product quality (measure, rhythm — `foundations/typography.md`); dyslexia-aware choices (no justified text, generous spacing).
- **Imagery:** real learners and real instructors (credentials visible); course cards with consistent thumbnail art systems; screenshots of actual lesson UX for self-paced products.
- **Theme:** light default; dark mode genuinely valuable for night-study products — do it properly or not at all.

## Page priorities & patterns
1. **Course/program cards:** thumbnail → title (outcome-phrased: "Build your first API") → instructor + credential → meta row (duration, level, rating count) → price/included-in-plan chip
2. **Course detail:** outcome list ("you will be able to…") → syllabus accordion (lesson titles visible — the content IS the proof; free-preview lessons marked) → instructor bio with authority (`psychology/persuasion.md`) → reviews → sticky enroll box (price, guarantee, cohort date)
3. **Learning dashboard:** resume-where-you-left as the PRIMARY element ("Continue: Lesson 4 — 12 min left"), progress per course (bars, honest %), streak/next-milestone (goal-gradient — `psychology/cognition.md`), due dates calm not alarming
4. **Lesson player:** distraction-free (chrome recedes), progress within lesson, notes/transcript access, next-lesson momentum (auto-advance with cancel), completion moment (licensed micro-celebration — `components/states.md`)
5. **Certificates/outcomes:** shareable, verifiable (URL), designed like documents worth sharing

## Trust requirements
Instructor credentials that verify · honest outcome data (completion, placement — with
methodology) · accreditation/partnership marks (real) · transparent pricing incl. what
"lifetime access" means · refund policy in one sentence · syllabus depth visible pre-purchase.

## Motion character
Friendly preset: warm micro-feedback, progress animations (bar fills 300–400ms), earned
celebrations at real milestones (course complete: yes; video watched: no). Gamification
ethics: streaks motivate but never shame — "streak freeze" mercy mechanics beat guilt copy.

## Accessibility note
This sector's legal and moral bar is highest: captions on ALL video, transcripts, keyboard-
complete lesson players, WCAG AA minimum with AAA targets for content (`foundations/accessibility.md`).

## Anti-patterns
Fake enrollment counters/countdown discounts (Udemy-style perpetual "90% off" trains
distrust) · locked syllabus ("buy to see what you'll learn") · progress bars that lie ·
infantilizing adult learners · certificate mills aesthetics (gold seals, comic ribbons).

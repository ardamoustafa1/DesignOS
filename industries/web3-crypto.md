# Industry: Web3 & Crypto

## Buyer psychology
The most trust-damaged sector in software: every visitor has read about rug pulls, hacks,
and collapses. Audiences split hard — **degens** (speed, alpha, native slang tolerance),
**builders** (docs, audits, decentralization reality), and **institutions/normies**
(regulatory posture, custody, "is this a casino?"). Pick the audience per product and
design for their specific skepticism; a page serving all three serves none
(`psychology/trust.md` ledger: this sector starts deeply overdrawn).

## Visual language
- **The 2021 aesthetic is a scarlet letter:** neon-purple gradients, floating 3D coins,
  laser-eyed apes, "to the moon" energy now signal exactly the projects that died.
  Post-collapse credibility dresses like infrastructure: `industries/analytics-hosting.md`
  monochrome discipline, real typography, engineering aesthetics (Uniswap/Base-era
  restraint with one confident brand gesture).
- **Color:** dark-first native; restrained accents; semantic red/green price conventions
  respected AND localized (`industries/fintech.md` CN-flip note).
- **Type:** grotesque + heavy mono for the sector's real textures: addresses (truncated
  middle `0x1a2b…9f8e` + copy button — `components/code-blocks.md` copy doctrine),
  hashes, amounts (tabular-nums, precision rules per asset recorded in `memory/design.md`).
- **Imagery:** product UIs, architecture diagrams, honest charts (`components/charts.md`
  honesty rules are legally load-bearing here). NFT/consumer products may run art-forward —
  the art is the product then.

## Page priorities & patterns
1. **Landing:** what it does in normie-translatable language + the live product; TVL/
   volume stats only if real, sourced, live-linked (on-chain verifiable numbers are the
   sector's unique trust asset — USE that: "verify on Etherscan" links)
2. **Security page as the conversion page:** audits (named firms, linked reports, dates),
   bug bounty, multisig/custody model, admin-key reality (who can rug, technically —
   honesty here is the differentiator), incident history
3. **Docs** (`patterns/docs-sites.md`): builders evaluate docs before token; contract
   addresses in copy-safe blocks, testnet quickstarts
4. **The dApp itself:** wallet-connect flows (the sector's auth — `patterns/onboarding-auth.md`
   adapted: network states, wrong-chain prompts, transaction states designed:
   pending/confirmed/failed with explorer links, gas transparency BEFORE signing;
   `components/states.md` at maximum — a spinner on a $50k transaction is negligence)
5. Tokenomics/governance: charts honest, unlock schedules visible, voting UX real

## Trust requirements
Named team beats anon for anything touching custody (anon-credible only in pure-protocol
culture, and even there: track record) · audits linked not claimed · regulatory posture
stated per market · risk disclosures designed-in, not fine-print buried
(`industries/fintech.md` disclaimer layer) · NO yield promises without mechanism +
risk framing (regulatorily radioactive and ethically instant-fail).

## Motion character
Precise-technical; licensed signatures: live on-chain data ticking (real), transaction-
state choreography. BAN: number-go-up casino energy, confetti on trades
(`psychology/gamification.md` ethics gate — gamified trading harmed people; regulators
noticed).

## Anti-patterns
Moon/rocket/laser anything · APY in 72pt with the risk in 7pt · fake/unlinked audit
badges · countdown token sales pressure · "not financial advice" as a joke while the
whole page is financial advice · wallet-drainer-lookalike UX (surprise signature
requests, unlabeled approvals — design AGAINST the scam patterns users fear: every
signature request explains itself).

# Industry: Media & News

## Buyer psychology
Two masters, structurally at war: **readers** (get me the story, fast, without ambush)
and **the business** (subscriptions/ads need attention captured). Every great news design
is a treaty between them; every hated one is the business winning too visibly. Reader
trust is the only durable asset — and it's bought with restraint: clean typography,
honest headlines, labeled sponsorship, fast pages (`psychology/trust.md` ledger applies
to journalism doubly).

## Visual language
- **Typography IS the brand:** news lives and dies on type systems — a serif headline
  voice (authority: Tiempos/Publico class) or sharp grotesque (digital-native: Guardian/
  Axios energy), rigorous hierarchy scale, and the discipline to never decorate what
  should inform (`foundations/typography.md` at maximum, article measure sacred).
- **Color:** restrained chrome — near-black text, white/warm-paper ground, ONE brand
  accent (the sector's reds/blues carry mastheads); section colors only if systematized;
  data-viz palettes designed (`components/charts.md` — news graphics are trust surfaces).
- **Imagery:** photojournalism honored with size and captions/credits (always — credits
  are ethics made visible); NO decorative stock beside reporting.

## Page priorities & patterns
1. **The front page:** editorial hierarchy made physical (`foundations/layout.md` visual
   weight = editorial judgment): one lead story unmistakably dominant, 2–3 secondary,
   ranked rivers below — a front page of equal cards abdicates the editor's job.
   Curation beats chronology above the fold.
2. **The article** (`patterns/blog-content.md` + news specifics): headline (honest, no
   curiosity-gap bait) → standfirst → byline + REAL timestamp (published AND updated —
   correction transparency) → body with the measure/rhythm doctrine → related-by-story
   (not clickbait-related) → comments if moderated.
3. **Live coverage:** reverse-chron update stream, pinned summary on top ("what we know"),
   timestamped entries, auto-update with a "3 new updates" pill (never reflow under the
   reader — `components/notifications.md` arrival doctrine).
4. **Section pages, search, archives:** navigable depth — news sites are libraries wearing
   storefronts (`components/search-command.md`, real pagination).

## The monetization treaty
- **Paywalls designed honestly:** metered/freemium states clear BEFORE the click where
  possible, the wall itself a designed moment (what you get, price, one CTA) — not a
  scroll-jacking curtain dropped mid-paragraph. Subscriber value visible (fewer ads,
  exclusive depth).
- **Ads contained:** fixed slots that never shift layout (`checklists/performance.md`
  CLS — news sites are the world's worst CLS offenders and it's the ad integration),
  labeled clearly, NEVER styled as content. Sponsored content: unmissable labeling —
  one disguised advertorial spends years of masthead trust.
- Newsletter capture per `patterns/blog-content.md` rules (the sector's real retention
  engine — `psychology/habit-retention.md` cadence honesty: daily brief products).

## Speed & accessibility (editorial values, technically expressed)
News = worst networks, oldest devices, screen readers, breaking-moment traffic spikes:
text-first rendering, LCP = the headline not the hero ad, fonts subset + swap
(`checklists/performance.md` strictest tier). Captions/transcripts on video reporting
(`foundations/accessibility.md` — access to news is access to civic life).

## Anti-patterns
Curiosity-gap headlines ("You won't believe…") · interstitial ad walls · autoplay video
with sound · infinite-scroll article-to-article hijacking the back button · "around the
web" chumbox rows (instant credibility amputation) · timestamps hidden to make old news
look fresh · CLS from lazy ad slots · corrections made silently.

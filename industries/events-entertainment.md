# Industry: Events & Entertainment

## Buyer psychology
Events are bought as **anticipated memories**: the ticket is a promise of a feeling
(the drop, the keynote, the curtain). Purchase energy is high but fragile — hype converts,
but the moment logistics get murky (real price, seat view, refund terms, "will I get in?"),
excitement flips to suspicion in seconds. The sector's design job: keep the hype and the
logistics in separate, both-excellent registers (`industries/travel-hospitality.md`
dream/logistics doctrine, compressed into minutes).

## Visual language
- **The event's identity IS the design system** (`industries/gaming.md` doctrine): a jazz
  festival, a dev conference, and a theater season must not share a template — extract
  palette/type/motion from the event's world into `memory/brand.md` per event.
- **Color:** high-energy licensed (gradients, saturated accents, dark-stage aesthetics
  for music/nightlife); conferences: brand-forward but content-legible; theater/arts:
  editorial restraint with one dramatic gesture.
- **Type:** expressive display faces at their most licensed — the poster tradition lives
  here (huge dates, huge names); UI layer stays disciplined underneath
  (`foundations/typography.md` floors).
- **Imagery:** last edition's REAL footage/photos are the conversion engine (atmosphere
  proof beats lineup lists); first editions: venue + artist assets, honestly labeled.

## Page priorities & patterns
1. **The event hero:** name, date(s), venue+city, and ONE action (Get tickets) —
   answerable in 3 seconds (`components/hero.md`); countdown licensed HERE (a real date
   approaches — the sector's one honest countdown) but calm, not casino.
2. **Lineup/program/speakers:** the content proof — cards with faces (bios expandable),
   schedule as a designed timetable (tracks × time grid, filterable, personal-agenda
   builders for conferences; `components/tables.md` responsive strategies for the grid),
   changes communicated (replaced speakers noted honestly).
3. **Tickets — the trust machine:** tier cards (`patterns/pricing.md` mechanics: what
   each tier includes, honest availability), TOTAL price with fees up front (fee-ambush
   is the sector's Ticketmaster-shaped trauma — all-in pricing is the differentiator and
   increasingly law), seat maps where seated (view-from-seat where possible), refund/
   transfer terms AT the button, queue/waiting-room states designed
   (`components/states.md`: position honesty, no fake scarcity while inventory sits).
4. **Post-purchase:** the confirmation is the pre-event product — wallet passes, calendar
   files, know-before-you-go (doors, bag policy, transit), lineup-change notifications
   (`patterns/email-templates.md` transactional discipline).
5. **Post-event:** the memory + next-edition funnel — aftermovie, photo galleries,
   "sign up for next year" (the retention moment — `psychology/habit-retention.md`).

## Trust requirements
Organizer identity + history visible · real availability (fake "selling fast" =
`psychology/persuasion.md` instant-fail; REAL urgency is this sector's native truth —
just don't counterfeit it) · secure-checkout signals · anti-scalping/official-reseller
clarity · accessibility info as first-class content (venue access, seating, services —
legally required and morally loud).

## Motion character
Maximum license with the gaming rules (`industries/gaming.md`): energy in the hype layer
(hero video loops, lineup reveals, one signature scroll moment), ZERO decoration in the
ticket machine — checkout is `industries/ecommerce.md` sacred ground. Reduced-motion
fallbacks total; strobe-adjacent effects banned (`foundations/accessibility.md` —
this sector's imagery flirts with the seizure line; audit hard).

## Anti-patterns
Fee ambush at step 4 · countdown timers on non-deadlines · lineup in an image (unsearchable,
unzoomable) · autoplay with sound · hype site with no date/venue above the fold · seat
maps that hide obstructed views · post-purchase silence until event day · fake queues.

# Industry: Insurance

## Buyer psychology
Insurance is bought grudgingly (a bill against a hypothetical) and *experienced* at the
worst moments of a customer's life (the crash, the flood, the diagnosis). Two design
registers, never blended: **purchase** = clarity against confusion-fatigue (the sector
trained users to expect fine-print ambushes), **claims** = calm competence for people
in distress (`industries/healthcare.md` stressed-user doctrine applies whole).
The quote funnel is where insurtech disrupted incumbents — speed-to-number IS the pitch.

## Visual language
- **Color:** trust blues/greens with warm humanity accents; insurtech may modernize
  (Lemonade-class warmth) but claims surfaces stay calm-neutral — never alarm-red
  ambient (`psychology/color-psychology.md` thermostat, at a claims moment especially).
- **Type:** high-legibility sans, generous sizes (broad demographic), plain-language
  law: policy content translated to 8th-grade reading with legal text linked, not
  leading (`industries/legal.md` disclaimer-layer craft).
- **Imagery:** real life being lived (homes, roads, families) — protective, not
  fear-based; NO crash/disaster imagery in purchase flows (fear converts poorly and
  cheaply here); diagrams for coverage explanation beat prose walls.

## Page priorities & patterns
1. **The quote funnel** (`components/steppers-wizards.md` at full depth): minimal-field
   steps, instant estimate as early as honest ("from ₺X/mo — refines as we learn more"),
   progress honesty, save-and-resume (quotes are comparison-shopped across sessions),
   the "why we ask" note on every personal question (`psychology/trust.md`
   point-of-disclosure).
2. **Coverage explanation:** the covered/not-covered table designed honestly
   (`patterns/comparison-pages.md` no-red-✗ rule; exclusions VISIBLE, plainly worded —
   hidden exclusions are the sector's trust bankruptcy and increasingly regulator bait),
   scenario cards ("tree falls on car → covered under X").
3. **Pricing/tiers:** `patterns/pricing.md` mechanics; deductible↔premium sliders with
   live math (tabular-nums, `components/pickers.md` slider craft); total honesty on
   fees/adjustments.
4. **The claims flow — the real product:** one obvious "File a claim" from everywhere;
   `components/file-upload.md` mercy at maximum (photos from a roadside, on mobile,
   stressed); status transparency (received → assessed → decision → paid, with dates —
   `components/states.md`); human escape hatch visible at every step (claims are where
   phone numbers earn their pixels).
5. **Policy documents:** `patterns/print-documents.md` — the PDF users actually file;
   renewal/change notices designed, not generated-and-forgotten.

## Trust requirements
License/regulatory numbers per market · claims-payment stats where strong ("94% of
claims paid within 5 days" — sourced) · real reviews including claim experiences ·
transparent renewal pricing (the loyalty-penalty pattern is regulated in growing
markets and hated everywhere) · what-happens-next clarity at purchase (documents,
cooling-off period, first steps).

## Motion character
Calm preset throughout; quote-funnel progress may warm slightly; claims surfaces:
near-static, instant feedback only. Zero celebration on purchase completion beyond
sober confirmation (buying insurance isn't a party); the licensed warm moment: claim
PAID.

## Anti-patterns
Fear-marketing imagery · quote funnels demanding phone/email before ANY number (lead-gen
ambush = instant bounce) · exclusions in 7pt · dark-pattern auto-renewal · claims flows
buried three menus deep · chipper copy on claim denials (`psychology/emotional-design.md`
personality-off surfaces) · fake urgency on policies.

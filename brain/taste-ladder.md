# Taste Ladder

Use this ladder when a screen is already correct but still not exceptional. The goal is
not "more visual effects"; the goal is higher signal, clearer hierarchy, and a design
language that could not be swapped onto any other product unchanged.

## Level 70: Functional

- The page has the required sections.
- It uses readable type and working controls.
- It could have been produced by almost any template.
- Failure mode: complete, but forgettable.

## Level 80: Clean

- Spacing is consistent.
- Colors are restrained.
- The primary CTA is visible.
- Failure mode: polished generic SaaS. Logo swap test usually fails.

## Level 90: Credible

- The surface shows product understanding.
- Claims are concrete and honest.
- The page has realistic states, edge cases, and interaction affordances.
- Failure mode: trustworthy but not memorable.

## Level 95: Premium

- One clear signature vehicle is derived from the product material.
- First viewport answers: what is it, who is it for, why this, what next.
- The page contains an inspectable artifact: screenshot, workflow, table, code, report,
  map, feed, timeline, or demo state.
- Section rhythm varies by meaning, not decoration.
- Proof is sourced, transparent, or replaced with architecture/economic proof.

## Level 99: Category-Defining

- The design creates a reusable language, not a one-page stunt.
- A user can describe the page from memory the next day.
- Every major decision has a product reason.
- The design is distinctive without taxing accessibility, speed, or comprehension.
- It contains at least one moment competitors would want to copy.

## Upgrade Moves

When stuck at 80:

- Replace generic adjectives with concrete product mechanics.
- Add a real product artifact above the fold.
- Reduce repeated card grids; vary section composition by job.
- Pick one signature vehicle from `brain/originality.md`.
- Show one non-happy-path state.

When stuck at 90:

- Strengthen the signature vehicle and repeat it subtly across the page.
- Make proof more inspectable: table, source, benchmark, artifact, or caveat.
- Tighten copy until every sentence changes a buyer's understanding.
- Remove any section that does not increase confidence or action.

When stuck at 95:

- Run the swap test.
- Run the squint, glance, minute test from `brain/quality-bar.md`.
- Compare against the closest reference pack and name the one gap still visible.
- Ask what the product's material wants to become visually.

## Anti-patterns

- More gradients instead of more product truth.
- More sections instead of sharper hierarchy.
- Invented proof instead of honest proof.
- Trend stacking as personality.
- Uniform card rhythm across the entire page.
- A signature moment that appears once and never becomes a system.

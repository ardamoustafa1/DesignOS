# Originality — The Signature Discipline

DesignOS's rules produce *correct* design; this module is how correct becomes
*unmistakable*. The quality bar's hardest test isn't craft — it's the creative director's
question #3: "would this work with any logo swapped in?" (`agents/creative-director.md`).
This is the system for answering no.

## The economics of boldness
Originality is a budget, not a temperature. The formula the whole system enforces:
**95% discipline + 5% signature, placed deliberately.**
- All-discipline = competent and forgettable (the "clean but generic" review verdict).
- All-bold = chaos wearing a mood board.
- The 5% placed on the wrong element (an experimental nav) = expensive confusion;
  placed right (a memorable hero, a distinctive type voice) = identity at almost no
  usability cost. **Innovate in the content layers; standardize the chrome**
  (`psychology/cognition.md` Jakob doctrine).

## Where signatures live (pick 1–2 per brand, record in memory/brand.md)
| Signature vehicle | Cost | Memorability |
|---|---|---|
| **Type voice** (a distinctive display face, a scale with drama) | low | very high — the cheapest identity |
| **Color stance** (an unexpected-for-sector palette, executed with discipline) | low | high (`psychology/color-psychology.md` procedure step 3) |
| **The signature moment** (one designed interaction/animation) | medium | high (`motion/principles.md` whitelist slot) |
| **Illustration/image system** (a drawn voice, a photographic grade) | high | very high, ages well |
| **Layout gesture** (a recurring compositional move — the offset column, the oversized number) | low | medium — compounds across pages |
| **Voice/copy** (how it SOUNDS — Oatly-class) | low | very high, hardest to fake |
| Novel navigation/interaction paradigms | very high | usually negative — expert license only |

## The derivation method (original ≠ invented from nothing)
Originality is honest derivation from the SPECIFIC, which templates can't have:
1. **Derive from the product's material.** What does this product actually look like,
   produce, or process? (Relay's timeline became its hero — the walkthrough's amber
   signal derived from alert-semantics, `examples/saas-landing-walkthrough.md`.) The
   design language hiding inside the product's own data/artifacts is uncopyable.
2. **Derive from the audience's world,** not the sector's template: what does THIS
   buyer's day look like, what texture is credible to them
   (`industries/` psychology sections are derivation mines).
3. **Derive from the brand's constraint or belief:** a real opinion ("we think meetings
   are broken") renders visually in ways a value-neutral template can't.
4. **Cross-pollinate distant references:** the SaaS site borrowing from editorial print,
   the fintech borrowing from swiss timetables — one distant import, fully digested
   (`brain/reference-library.md` usage rule: steal tactics, never pixels — and steal
   from OUTSIDE the sector for originality).

## Tests before shipping a signature
- **The swap test:** logo swapped — does the design still clearly belong to this brand?
- **The squint-memory test:** a day later, can you describe what made it THIS product's
  page? ("it was clean" = fail; "the amber timeline" = pass)
- **The cost test:** does the signature tax usability, performance, or a11y ANYWHERE?
  Signatures pay rent in identity but never borrow from the priority stack
  (`brain/decision-framework.md` — beauty never buys back usability, and neither does
  novelty).
- **The repetition test:** does it survive appearing on every page? A signature is a
  system element, not a one-page stunt — if it only works once, it's a moment, not an
  identity (both are fine; know which you built).

## Anti-patterns (fake originality)
- Trend-stacking as identity (three current-shelf trends ≠ a signature —
  `brain/trend-radar.md` discipline)
- The viral-portfolio clone ("distinctive" copied is template with extra steps —
  `industries/portfolio-agency.md`)
- Random weirdness ("make it unexpected" without derivation — confusion cosplaying as
  creativity)
- Signature inflation: five signatures = zero signatures (the budget is 1–2)
- Novelty in the money paths (checkout, auth, settings innovate NEVER —
  `industries/ecommerce.md` sacred-ground doctrine)

- [ ] All four tests passed (swap, memory, cost, repetition)

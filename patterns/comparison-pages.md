# Comparison & Alternative Pages

"X vs Y" and "X alternative" pages capture the highest-intent traffic that exists —
users mid-decision, cards in hand. They're also where credibility is easiest to torch:
one strawman row and the reader trusts nothing else on your site.

## The honesty doctrine (this page's physics)
Readers arrive PRE-SKEPTICAL — they know you wrote the scorecard. The counterintuitive
winning move is **conceding real ground**: "If you need X, they're genuinely stronger.
Here's where we win instead." Concession converts skeptics; sweep-the-board tables convert
nobody who was actually comparing (`psychology/persuasion.md` authority through honesty).

## Page spine
```
1. HERO        positioning sentence: who each product is FOR — the segmentation claim
               ("Both are great. We're built for small teams; they're built for
               enterprise IT.") + primary CTA
2. TL;DR       the 3-bullet verdict box: choose us if… / choose them if… (yes, really)
3. TABLE       the feature comparison (below)
4. DEPTH       2–3 sections expanding YOUR differentiators — with product evidence
               (screenshots/demos), not adjectives
5. MIGRATION   "Switching from Y" — importer, timeline, what maps to what
               (industries/crm-erp.md migration doctrine; this section converts)
6. PROOF       testimonials FROM SWITCHERS specifically ("We moved from Y because…")
7. PRICING     honest side-by-side of comparable tiers, dated ("their pricing as of
               Jul 2026, per their site" — sourced, or omitted)
8. FAQ + CTA   the vs-objections; final band
```

## The comparison table
- Rows = user JOBS, not your feature names ("Automate handoffs" not "FlowBuilder™") —
  the reader knows their jobs, not your nouns.
- Cells: ✓ / — / short values ("Unlimited" / "5 seats") · never red ✗ (absence isn't
  error — `patterns/pricing.md` table rules) · footnote-link anything arguable.
- **Their column must be accurate and current**: date-stamp it, source it, correct it
  when they ship — a stale "they don't have dark mode" (they do) detonates the page.
- Include rows you LOSE. 3–4 honest losses buy belief in your 12 wins.
- Sticky headers, mobile strategy per `components/tables.md`; your column highlighted
  subtly (accent border, not a spotlight opera).

## Alternative-pages variant ("Y alternatives")
Listicle format: you first (declared honestly — "we made this list, here's our bias"),
then 4–6 REAL alternatives each with a fair one-liner + who-it's-for. The generosity is
the strategy: the page ranks, the fairness converts, and readers remember who told the
truth. Zero-fairness versions read as ads and bounce.

## Legal & tonal guardrails
- Competitor names in claims: factual, sourced, current (comparative advertising law
  varies by market — factual claims with sources are the safe and persuasive lane).
- No trademark logos in your table header without checking use policy; text names are safe.
- Tone: respectful-confident. Mockery reads as insecurity and screenshots virally.
- Never fabricate their weaknesses or their pricing; if unverifiable, omit.

## SEO notes (`checklists/seo.md`)
One page per competitor (real intent: "notion vs linear"); title = the exact query;
your TL;DR box targets the featured snippet; FAQ markup on the vs-questions; keep pages
updated — comparison SERPs punish staleness, and competitors' pages about YOU are the
alternative result.

## Anti-patterns
- The all-green home column vs their wall of red ✗ (nobody believes it, everybody bounces)
- Comparing your Pro tier against their Free tier silently
- Feature rows only you would name ("Proprietary SynergyMesh: ✓ / ✗")
- Stale competitor data · unsourced pricing · mocking tone
- Burying the migration story (the reader's real question is "how painful is switching")

## Checklist
- [ ] Segmentation claim in hero; TL;DR box with genuine choose-them cases
- [ ] Table: job-named rows, sourced+dated their-column, visible losses, no red ✗
- [ ] Differentiators shown with product evidence; switcher testimonials
- [ ] Migration section with real importer/timeline
- [ ] Legal lane: factual, sourced, current; tone respectful
- [ ] One page per competitor, snippet-targeted, update cadence in memory/todo.md
